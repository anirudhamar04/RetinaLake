# 1000x39

## Overview
The 1000x39 dataset consists of 1000 retinal fundus images belonging to 39 distinct classes. 

All images originate from the Joint Shantou International Eye Centre (JSIEC), located in Shantou city, Guangdong province, China. 
The dataset represents a small subset of a larger internal collection of 209,494 fundus images intended for training, validation, and testing of a deep learning platform. 
The copyright of all images belongs to JSIEC.

## Images
- The dataset contains color fundus photographs.
- All images are stored in **JPG** format.
- No information is provided regarding image resolution, field of view, camera model, or acquisition protocol.
- Image-level labels are not embedded in filenames; class information is provided through the directory structure.
## Metadata
No separate CSV or tabular metadata files are provided with this dataset. 
All metadata labels correspond directly to the folder names in which the images are stored. 
The folder structure itself serves as the sole source of class annotation, and labels must be inferred explicitly from the directory names.
- **0.0.Normal** – Fundus images of clinically normal eyes with no observable retinal or optic disc pathology.
- **0.1.Tessellated fundus** – Images showing tessellation of the choroidal vessels, typically associated with thin retinal pigment epithelium, often seen in myopia.
- **0.2.Large optic cup** – Fundus images characterized by an abnormally large optic cup-to-disc ratio, which may be a risk factor for glaucoma.
- **0.3.DR1** – Images showing mild diabetic retinopathy (DR), typically including microaneurysms without more advanced lesions.
- **1.0.DR2** – Images corresponding to moderate diabetic retinopathy, with findings such as increased microaneurysms, hemorrhages, or hard exudates.
- **1.1.DR3** – Images showing severe diabetic retinopathy, characterized by extensive hemorrhages, venous beading, or intraretinal microvascular abnormalities.
- **10.0.Possible glaucoma** – Fundus images suspicious for glaucoma, often showing optic disc cupping, rim thinning, or asymmetry.
- **10.1.Optic atrophy** – Images with pallor of the optic disc indicating optic nerve damage or degeneration.
- **11.Severe hypertensive retinopathy** – Fundus images showing advanced retinal changes due to hypertension, such as hemorrhages, exudates, or optic disc edema.
- **12.Disc swelling and elevation** – Images exhibiting optic disc edema or elevation, potentially due to papilledema or inflammatory conditions.
- **13.Dragged Disc** – Fundus images where the optic disc appears displaced or distorted, often due to traction from retinal disease.
- **14.Congenital disc abnormality** – Images showing congenital anomalies of the optic disc, such as tilted disc or optic disc coloboma.
- **15.0.Retinitis pigmentosa** – Images depicting retinal degeneration consistent with retinitis pigmentosa, including vessel attenuation and peripheral pigmentation.
- **15.1.Bietti crystalline dystrophy** – Fundus images with crystalline deposits in the retina, characteristic of Bietti crystalline dystrophy.
- **16.Peripheral retinal degeneration and break** – Images showing degenerative changes or breaks in the peripheral retina, which may predispose to retinal detachment.
- **17.Myelinated nerve fiber** – Fundus images containing visible myelinated retinal nerve fiber layers, appearing as white opaque patches.
- **18.Vitreous particles** – Images showing opacities or floaters within the vitreous body.
- **19.Fundus neoplasm** – Images containing intraocular tumors or mass lesions visible on fundus examination.
- **2.0.BRVO** – Fundus images of branch retinal vein occlusion, typically showing sectoral hemorrhages and venous dilation.
- **2.1.CRVO** – Images of central retinal vein occlusion, characterized by widespread hemorrhages and venous congestion.
- **20.Massive hard exudates** – Images with extensive hard exudate deposits, often associated with diabetic macular involvement.
- **21.Yellow-white spots-flecks** – Fundus images showing scattered yellow-white lesions or flecks, which may indicate inherited or inflammatory retinal disorders.
- **22.Cotton-wool spots** – Images containing cotton-wool spots, representing localized retinal nerve fiber layer infarcts.
- **23.Vessel tortuosity** – Fundus images where retinal vessels appear abnormally twisted or tortuous.
- **24.Chorioretinal atrophy-coloboma** – Images showing chorioretinal atrophy or colobomatous defects involving the retina and choroid.
- **25.Preretinal hemorrhage** – Images with hemorrhage located anterior to the retina, often boat-shaped in appearance.
- **26.Fibrosis** – Fundus images demonstrating fibrotic tissue formation, commonly due to chronic retinal disease or post-treatment scarring.
- **27.Laser Spots** – Images showing retinal laser photocoagulation scars from prior therapeutic intervention.
- **28.Silicon oil in eye** – Fundus images with visible silicone oil droplets, typically following vitreoretinal surgery.
- **29.0.Blur fundus without PDR** – Blurred or low-quality fundus images without evidence of proliferative diabetic retinopathy.
- **29.1.Blur fundus with suspected PDR** – Blurred fundus images where proliferative diabetic retinopathy is suspected but not clearly visible.
- **3.RAO** – Images of retinal artery occlusion, showing retinal whitening and attenuated arteries.
- **4.Rhegmatogenous RD** – Fundus images of rhegmatogenous retinal detachment, typically showing elevated, corrugated retina.
- **5.0.CSCR** – Images showing central serous chorioretinopathy, often with localized serous retinal detachment.
- **5.1.VKH disease** – Fundus images associated with Vogt–Koyanagi–Harada disease, including exudative retinal detachments.
- **6.Maculopathy** – Images depicting macular pathology affecting the central retina.
- **7.ERM** – Fundus images showing epiretinal membrane formation on the macular surface.
- **8.MH** – Images of macular hole, characterized by a full-thickness defect at the fovea.
- **9.Pathological myopia** – Fundus images of high or pathological myopia, including posterior staphyloma, lacquer cracks, or chorioretinal atrophy.

## Storage in database

### Tables populated

- **`datasets`** — One row registered with `dataset_name="1000x39"`, `source_url`, `license="CC-BY-4.0"`, and `modality_types=["fundus"]`.
- **`images`** — One row per image. Fields populated via `get_image_metadata_dict` plus `modality="fundus"` and `original_image_id` set to the image filename stem. Images are discovered by walking all 39 class folders for `.jpg` / `.JPG` / `.jpeg` / `.JPEG` files. No laterality is stored.
- **`disease_grading`** — One row per image in the 4 DR folders only (`0.0.Normal`, `0.3.DR1`, `1.0.DR2`, `1.1.DR3`). Grade values and scale:
  - Scale name: `1000x39_DR_0_3` (0=No DR (Normal), 1=DR1, 2=DR2, 3=DR3; min=0, max=3).
  - Note: DR images receive a `disease_grading` record **instead of** a `classification_annotations` record.
  - `annotation_method="manual"`.
- **`classification_annotations`** — One row per image in the 35 non-DR folders. Each annotation uses `task_type="binary"`, `class_value=True` (presence), and `class_name` set to the folder's snake_case disease identifier (e.g., `"tessellated_fundus"`, `"brvo"`, `"macular_hole"`). `annotation_method="manual"`.
- **`keyword_annotations`** — One or more rows per image for all 39 folders. Keywords are derived from the `FOLDER_ANNOTATIONS` mapping and stored with `keyword_source="diagnostic_keywords"` and `annotation_method="manual"`. Example keyword lists per folder:
  - `0.0.Normal` → `["normal"]`
  - `2.0.BRVO` → `["BRVO", "branch retinal vein occlusion"]`
  - `8.MH` → `["MH", "macular hole"]`
  - Keywords are inserted individually (no bulk operation).

### Provenance / raw annotation files

This dataset has no separate annotation files. The folder tree is traversed via `process_folder_tree`, which registers provenance per image file at ingest time. Each image's annotation type is set to `"classification"` (primary type for the folder tree traversal).

### Splits

No splits are created. The dataset provides no train/test partition and the script does not call `register_standard_splits`.

### Special processing

- The folder structure is walked recursively. Each image is matched to its parent folder name, which must be present in the `FOLDER_ANNOTATIONS` mapping; unknown folders are skipped with an error.
- DR and non-DR images are annotated in mutually exclusive tables: DR folders write to `disease_grading` only; non-DR folders write to `classification_annotations` only. All folders write to `keyword_annotations`.
- Bulk upserts are used for images, gradings, and classifications. Keyword annotations are individually upserted.
- The script is idempotent: re-running does not create duplicates due to deterministic UUID generation and upsert logic.
